Alumni News Update 12.7-12.13.21
Posted on December 6, 2021
Welcome to the weekly Alumni News Update! To have your news featured, please email schoolofartnews@cmu.edu with a brief description, link to more information, image, and your degree and class year. Or, add us to your newsletter list.
Lauren F. Adams’ MFA ’07 work is included in the traveling exhibition “Sally,” on view at SUNY Adirondack through Jan 28. 
Arden Bendler Browning BFA ’97 debuted a mural project at Yards Brewery in Philadelphia.
Mel Bochner BFA ’62 showed work with Simon Lee Gallery at Art Basel Miami Beach, Nov 30 – Dec 4. 
Paola Cabal BFA ‘98 was awarded the Marion Kryczka Excellence in Teaching Award in Painting and Drawing from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago. 
Matthew Constant BFA ’18 has a solo exhibition titled “very simple pictures” at Union Hall in Pittsburgh, opening Dec 9. 
Christopher Kardambikis BFA ‘05 participates in the Printed Matter and EXILE Books presentation at NADA Miami, Nov 30 – Dec 4.
Deborah Kass BFA ’74 showed work with Kavi Gupta Gallery at Art Basel Miami Beach, Nov 30 – Dec 4. 
Jenny Lee BFA ’00 showed work with Klaus von Nichtssagend Gallery at NADA Art Fair in Miami, Dec 1-4.
Dana Lok BFA ’11 showed work with Miguel Abreu Gallery at Art Basel Miami Beach, Nov 30 – Dec 4. 
Alex Lukas MFA ’18 exhibited work in the Printed Matter and EXILE Books presentation at NADA Miami, Nov 30 – Dec 4. 
Adam Milner MFA ’17 will be in conversation with Liz Magor in a virtual conversation on Dec 8 organized by Art21. 
Leah Piepgras MFA ’97 exhibits in the three-person show “Then or Now” at Take it Easy in Atlanta art gallery, opening Dec 11.
“Twenty Something,” edited by Amera Rizk BFA ’98, won best in Show at Siggraph Asia 2021 computer animation festival. 
Randall Rosenthal BFA ’69 showed work with Louis Meisel Gallery at Art Miami, Nov 30 – Dec 5.
Paul Rouphail MFA ’10 showed work with Smart Objects Gallery at NADA Art Fair in Miami, Dec 1-4.
Laura Sharp Wilson BFA ’87 exhibits in “Range” at Modern West in Salt Lake City, UT, through Jan 7.
