Weekly Alumni News Update 04.16-04.22.19
Posted on April 15, 2019Welcome to the Weekly Alumni News Update! To have your news featured, please email schoolofartnews@cmu.edu with a brief description, link to more information, image, and your degree and class year. Or, add us to your newsletter list.
Mark Baugh Sasaki BFA ’04 exhibits in the group exhibition “The Body Electric” at the Chinese Cultural Center San Francisco, opening Apr 17.
Sarah Marshall BFA ’92 was invited by the Red Press Collaborative at Youngstown State University to be a visiting artist. For the program, she made a limited edition print and gave an artists talk on Apr 8.
Rob Rogers MFA ’84 was named a finalist for the Pulitzer Prize in Editorial Cartooning, the second time in his career, he has been a finalist for the prestigious award.
Renée Stout BFA ‘80 participates in “Hedonist Buddhist” at the WPA Annex in Washington, D.C., through June 23.
Joni Sullivan BHA ’17 was accepted into the MFA program at Boston University for fall 2019.
Melissa Thorne BFA ’93 shows new works on paper in “Everyday Perfection” at the Albany International Airport, through Sept 2.
Zack Wallnau BFA ’11 was accepted in the AFI’s Conservatory program in Cinematography for fall 2019.
Moses Williams MFA ’17 was invited to serve as a Teaching Artist in Residence at Watkins College of Art in Nashville for the MFA low residency program’s 2019 summer term.
